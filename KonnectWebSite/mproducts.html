B.) Building Seismic Monitoring System (BSMS)


The BSMS is a system that provides earthquake instrumentation schemes that tracks and records how a building responds during seismic event occurrences. Data generated by the instrument can provide end users:


  1. an instant and crucial technical analysis reference data, in order to effectively strategise and implement emergency procedures;


  2. a ten (10) Second Seismic Early Warning and lead-time alarm to ensure that the building occupants shall be immediately informed and alerted;


  3. an overview to determine the extent of damage caused on the building by any violent shaking events, instantly aiding the building’s engineering team to decide on which areas of damage may need repair or reinforcement.

Command Centre



Konnect Universal Solutions Incorporated provides turnkey solutions for the planning, preparation, installation and commissioning of command centres and other field instruments for data gathering and analysis for key decisions.

As a central monitoring station, the Command Centre provides a pivotal point-of-view for decision makers, especially during emergencies. The system gives its users a bird’s eye view, continuously putting them on top of the situation.



Command Centre oversees the central management of day to day operations and control of civil operational functions in the event of natural or manmade calamities.

Key agencies such as the Police Department, Fire Department, Health Stations and other safety organizations are interconnected in to the system, ensuring the accurate exchange of information and swift mobilization of personnel including logistics are being done synchronously.


Local organizations down to the Barangay levels are ideally part of the system, since they are usually the first group or the first responder to the event.

CCTV cameras monitor what is happening in a particular area in real time, capturing videos and various still images around the clock, making authorities aware of the situation around and within the city. We offer a complete line of analogue,digital, hybrid and IP CCTV cameras for security and remote monitoring, depending on the client’s indoor and outdoor needs.



Aside from this, we can incorporate the aid of telemetry and other scientific instruments deployed in different locations - enabling the measurement of rainfall, temperature, humidity, wind speed and its direction - for analyses which can be used as a future point of comparison. Historical data can also be easily reviewed with just a few clicks.

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) – Map Explorer


For Real Property, Business Establishments and Tourism


Map Explorer is a web-based, GIS-enabled application that features interactive maps linked to a database. The MapExplorer can be linked to Business Permits and Licensing System (BPLS), Real Property Tax System (RPTS) and Tourism databases. The information from these databases is overlayed with GIS-map layers and can be manipulated using map tools.

The MapExplorer presents data and information in an attractive and highly visual manner. An advanced form of GIS, is capable of disseminating data, especially spatial data, widely over the internet for a wider audience.



Geographic Information Systems (GIS) – Electronic Business Permit and Licensing System (eBPLS)


electronic Business Permit and Licensing System


Electronic Business Permit and Licensing System (eBPLS) is a web-based application that enables Local Government Unit (LGUs) to process business applications electronically. The system standardizes the process flow in the application of business and licenses. It provides faster turn-around time in the processing of business transactions. The tedious manual processes that required applicant follow-ups will be replaced by a system that enables applications, queries on approval status, as well as remaining tax dues in real-time.

The collections on business permits and licenses, being the primary source of income of LGUs, lay the bench-mark from which the plans and programs of a city or municipality are established.



Geographic Information Systems (GIS) – Electronic Real Property Tax System (eRPTS)


electronic Real Property Tax System


Electronic Real Property Tax System (eRPTS) facilitates easy assessment of the real property, as well as improve the LGU's tax enforcement capabilities. the RPTS allows onscreen viewing, online printing and other transactions. Other features include separate searching for the parcel, building, machinery, and an option to save the result to CSV/Excel files.



Overview:


Users can compare the previous data to the current situation and readings from the field instruments with the rainfall recorded from a past typhoon event which caused flooding in a certain area. If there is similarity in reading, users can easily alarm and notify the areas of concern that possible flooding may occur any moment, or on a certain period of time.

Likewise, water levels in the open channels like rivers, dams, creeks, lakes and other water sheds can be measured automatically by Ultrasonic Water Level Sensors. This device can measure the water level and transmit the data back to command centre via broadband wireless or GSM/GPRS connection.

The transmitted data is analyzed, based on the configured alarm settings (i.e., LOW, MEDIUM, WARNING, and HIGH). From this alarm setting, the Command Centre can issue warnings to local officials - whether to stay or evacuate a certain area. Data gathered is being stored in a relational database located in the command centre. In a crucial situation, exchange of information should be precise and accurate - especially in a rugged environment.

We can also provide digital radio communication systems with a wider coverage, designed for extreme uses (like rescue operations) in all weather conditions. With this GPS enabled radio, the Command Centre can track the exact location and communicate this to the mobile units at the same time, enabling them to divert other units to different locations, when necessary.
